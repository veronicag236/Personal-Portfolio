COW: Converting
Organic Waste
Researcher · October 2025 – Present · UPenn CBE Lab
Biogas AI / Reinforcement Learning Sustainability UPenn

COW transforms campus food waste into renewable biogas using AI-optimized anaerobic digesters. Penn generates 17 lbs of food waste per meal per day — COW converts this into clean energy, fertilizer, and a living lab for students.

Food scraps are slurried and fed into a sealed tank where microbes break down organic material, producing biogas (mostly methane) stored in a flexible gas balloon. The system is modular and designed for replication across dining locations.

Our AI uses a Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) reinforcement learning agent to autonomously optimize temperature (37°C) and pH (7.0), achieving 23% greater efficiency and approximately half the energy usage compared to traditional PID control. A live dashboard displays system data for the Penn community.

We designed, built, and tested a small-scale biodigester prototype in the CBE lab, equipped with methane and temperature sensors showing steady increases in methane concentration. First at Penn to merge AI + anaerobic digestion for renewable energy.
